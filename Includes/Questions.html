Common Questions & Our Answers

If you didn't get your answer from this page, please send us your question to GlobalWarming@info.com

  • Is recycling really helping the planet?

    By reducing air and water pollution and saving energy, recycling offers an important environmental benefit: it reduces emissions of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide and chlorofluorocarbons, that contribute to global climate change.

  • What happens with the garbage we recycle? Where does it go? The paper and cardboard is transferred to a recycling plant that turns the waste into a new raw material for the production of paper products.
    The plastic bottles that are recycled goes to a plastic recycling plant and become a raw material for the production of plastic products such as: baskets, furniture and brass.
    The glass is transferred to a glass recycling plant for the production of new products.
    The packaging is stored, sorted according to the different types of materials and transferred to the various recycling plants.
    The electronic waste is transferred to a suitable recycling plant that breaks down the waste into an issue and transfers it for recycling to various plants.
    Textile waste, clothing and fabrics, are used for reuse and some are transferred to recycling plants for the production of rags and various fabric products.
